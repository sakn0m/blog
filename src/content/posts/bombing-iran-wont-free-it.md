---
title: bombing Iran won't free it
date: 2026-03-03
---

The logic seems almost intuitive: destroy the infrastructure of a regime, and the regime falls. It's the kind of reasoning that sounds clean in everyday talk, on TikTok, or in the talking points of those who've spent decades calling for "maximum pressure" on Tehran. But history and basic political theory tell a very different story.

What military strikes actually do, almost invariably, is consolidate authoritarian power. The Islamic Republic has survived forty-seven years not because Iranians love it, but because it has been extraordinarily effective at framing itself as the only barrier between the nation and foreign annihilation. Every missile that falls on Iranian soil is a gift to the Revolutionary Guards' propaganda apparatus. Every crater reinforces the narrative that the theocracy, however rotten from within, is a defensive necessity.

Look at what happened in Afghanistan, in Iraq, in Libya, in Syria. Look at what happened after the Iran-Iraq war, after every round of sanctions, after every round of assassinations and sabotage operations. The regime didn't weaken. It purged, it repressed, and it survived, because external pressure gives authoritarian systems a pretext to demand internal unity. The enemy outside becomes the justification for crushing the enemy inside. There is no reason to believe this time will be different.

And when the regime actually collapses, after months if not years of brutal war and slaughter? When the much-touted "regime change" happens? The country fractures into civil war. More people die. Militias fill the vacuum. Genocide often follows.

The people who actually threaten the Islamic Republic are the ones who took to the streets in 2019, in 2022, in 2025, who chanted *"zan, zendegi, azadi"* and *"death to the dictator"* at the cost of their lives. They don't need bombs. They need the world to stop giving the regime the tools of both repression and nationalist legitimacy.

European governments spent years condemning the regime's repression while IRGC-linked money flowed freely through their banks. Now, as bombs from their cherished allies strike Iranian cities, burying children under the rubble of their schools, they issue careful statements about 'maximum restraint' and the security of the Strait, and then quietly provide the military cover, the bases, the intelligence partnerships, and the political silence that keep the machine running. Not because they believe in the freedom of the Iranian people. Because their whole economic architecture depends on the perpetuation of stability and the free flow of Gulf oil.

It is also worth being precise about who is leading these strikes, and what their track record looks like. Israel is a state currently facing proceedings at the International Court of Justice for genocide in Gaza, a campaign that has killed tens of thousands of civilians, starved an entire population, and systematically destroyed hospitals, schools and refugee camps. 
This is not a state acting out of a principled commitment to human rights or regional stability. It is a state pursuing its own hegemonic interests under the cover of security language, backed by unconditional US military support regardless of what international law or the rubble of its previous interventions has to say about it. The idea that this same entity will now bring liberation to the Iranian people is not just naive, it is obscene. What Israel is doing in the broader region is not dismantling authoritarianism. It is demonstrating, in real time, what unchecked military power of a settler-colonial ethnostate looks like when it operates without accountability.

Bombing Iran doesn't end the regime. It entrenches it, radicalizes it, and gives it exactly the martyrdom narrative it needs to survive another generation. The Iranian people deserve better than being caught between a theocratic dictatorship and the logic of air power. Liberation, when it comes, will be built from within. The least the West can do is get out of the way.
